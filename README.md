## Project Overview

This Gaming and Mental Health Dashboard is a comprehensive data analysis project built in Microsoft Excel that examines the relationship between gaming habits and mental health indicators. The dashboard visualizes survey data from 1,000 respondents to reveal patterns in gaming behavior and their potential impacts on mental, physical, and social well-being.

---

## 🛠️ Tools Used

- **Microsoft Excel**
  - Pivot Tables for data aggregation
  - Pivot Charts for visualization
  - Slicers for interactive filtering
  - Conditional Formatting for highlighting trends
  - Formulas for calculations (SUMIFS, COUNTIFS, AVERAGE, etc.)
 
---

### Project Objectives
- Analyze gaming patterns across different demographics (age, gender)
- Identify correlations between gaming hours and mental health indicators
- Assess the impact of gaming on sleep quality, physical health, and social interactions
- Track addiction levels and their relationship with gaming preferences
- Provide actionable insights for gamers, parents, and mental health professionals

### Key Research Questions
1. How do gaming habits vary across different age groups and genders?
2. What is the relationship between gaming hours and mental health scores?
3. How does gaming addiction affect sleep patterns and physical health?
4. Which gaming platforms and genres are associated with higher engagement?
5. What impact does gaming have on academic/work productivity and social life?

### Target Audience
- Gamers - Understand personal gaming habits and potential impacts
- Parents/Educators - Monitor and guide healthy gaming behaviors
- Mental Health Professionals - Identify risk factors and intervention points
- Game Developers - Design games with player wellbeing in mind
- Researchers - Analyze gaming trends and mental health correlations

---

## Key Features

### Demographics Analysis
- Total Users: 1000 respondents
- Age Groups: Teens (13-17), Young Adults (18-24), Adults (25-34)
- Gender Distribution: Male, Female, Other

### Gaming Metrics
- Average daily gaming hours by gender
- Platform preferences (PC, Console, Mobile)
- Game genre popularity (FPS, MOBA, RPG, Mobile Games)
- Gaming addiction levels (High, Moderate, Low, Severe)

### Health Indicators
- Social isolation scores
- Face-to-face social hours per week
- Exercise hours weekly
- Physical symptoms (eye strain, back/neck pain)
- Sleep quality assessment

### Impact Metrics
- Academic/work productivity
- Physical weight changes
- Mood states correlation with gaming hours

## Key Findings

- Female gamers average 6.3 daily hours (highest among genders)
- Male gamers average 5.4 daily hours
- MOBA and FPS players show highest engagement (6.0-6.3 hours)
- Physical activity varies significantly by gender (1.5-7.7 hours/week)
- Eye strain and back pain correlate with gaming hours
- Sleep quality varies by gaming addiction level
- Young Adults (18-24) show highest gaming engagement

## Key Findings & Insights

### Gaming Hours Distribution

**Average Daily Gaming Hours by Gender**
| Gender | Average Hours |
|--------|---------------|
| Female | 5.97 |
| Male | 6.23 |
| Other | 6.63 |

**Insight:** Other gender category shows the highest average daily gaming time at 6.63 hours, followed closely by male gamers at 6.23 hours. Female gamers average 5.97 hours daily.

### Genre Preferences

**Average Hours by Game Genre**
| Genre | Average Hours |
|-------|---------------|
| FPS | 6.3 |
| Mobile Games | 6.3 |
| MOBA | 6.0 |
| RPG Strategy | 6.0 |

**Insight:** FPS and Mobile games show the highest engagement levels at 6.3 hours daily, suggesting these genres may be more immersive or habit-forming across all age groups and genders.

### Age Group Analysis

**Age Demographics**
| Age Group | Range |
|-----------|-------|
| Teen | 13-17 years |
| Young Adult | 18-24 years |
| Adult | 25-34 years |
| Middle Age | 35-50 years |

**Gaming Patterns by Age Group**

| Age Group | Gaming Characteristics | Risk Factors | Protective Factors |
|-----------|------------------------|--------------|-------------------|
| Teen (13-17) | High engagement, developing habits | Identity formation, peer pressure | Parental guidance, school structure |
| Young Adult (18-24) | Peak gaming hours, highest addiction risk | Life transition, academic pressure | Social connections, career focus |
| Adult (25-34) | Balanced gaming, career responsibilities | Work-life balance, family duties | Established routines, financial stability |

**Insight:** Young adults (18-24) represent the highest risk demographic for gaming addiction, while middle-age adults (35-50) typically show more moderated gaming patterns focused on stress relief and entertainment rather than excessive engagement.

## Age-Specific Recommendations

### For Teens (13-17)
- Establish healthy gaming boundaries with parental involvement
- Balance gaming with physical activities and schoolwork
- Educate about ergonomics early to prevent future issues
- Monitor for sleep disruption from late-night gaming

### For Young Adults (18-24)
- Implement structured gaming schedules around work/study
- Address highest addiction risk through awareness programs
- Encourage physical activity maintenance (currently declining)
- Monitor for productivity impacts and sleep disorders

### For Adults (25-34)
- Maintain work-life-gaming balance
- Address emerging physical symptoms proactively
- Use gaming as stress relief, not escape
- Model healthy gaming for younger age groups

### For Middle Age (35-50)
- Focus on ergonomic setup to address back/neck pain
- Use gaming for stress management and family connection
- Maintain face-to-face social connections
- Monitor for moderate engagement patterns

---

## Cross-Generational Insights

1. **Gaming is Lifelong:** Engagement persists across all age groups, suggesting gaming is now a multi-generational activity.

2. **Risk Peaks in Young Adulthood:** The 18-24 age group requires most attention for addiction prevention and intervention.

3. **Physical Symptoms Evolve:** Eye strain affects all ages, while back pain becomes more prominent in middle age.

4. **Social Patterns Shift:** Online interaction peaks in young adulthood, gradually replaced by face-to-face connection in middle age.

5. **Maturation Effect:** Gaming habits naturally moderate with age, suggesting most young adults will develop healthier patterns over time.

6. **Platform Preferences Change:** Mobile gaming remains constant, while console/PC preferences shift with life stages.
